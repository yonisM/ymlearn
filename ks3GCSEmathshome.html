Module 1

1. The Value of Digits

Each digit in a given number has a place value. To better understand place value, numbers can be put in a place-value chart so that the value of each digit can be identified. For example in the number 683, the digit 6 is in the hundreds place and has a value of 600. The digit 8 is in the tens place and has a value of 80. The digit 3 is in the units place and has a value of 3.

2. Rounding

Rounding means making a number simpler but keeping its value close to what it was. The result is less accurate, but easier to use. Example: 73 rounded to the nearest ten is 70. In this lesson, you will learn how to round various degress of accuracy including to nearest units, 10, 100, 1000 .... nearest decimal place, and significant figures.

3. Powers and Roots

Powers are a way of indicating that a quantity is to be multiplied by itself some number of times i.e. 23 means 2 x 2 x 2. The Root of a number x is another number, which when multiplied by itself a given number of times, equals x. For example the second root of 9 is 3, because 3x3 = 9. The second root is usually called the square root. The third root is usually called the cube root.

4. Lowest Common Multiple (LCM) and Highest Common Factor (HCF)

Lowest Common Multiple (LCM) is the smallest positive number that is a multiple of two or more numbers. Highest Common Factor (HCF) is the highest number that can be divided exactly into each of two or more numbers.

6. Area

The area of a shape is the amount of space inside of a flat (2d shape). You'll be asked to find the area of various shapes including rectangle, squares, parallelograms, triangles, trapezium, shaded regions etc...

7. Volume

The volume of shape is the amount of space. It is measured in cubic units (3d shape) i.e cm3 or m3. Surface area is defined as the total area of the surface of a three-dimensional (3D) shape. In this topic, you'll be finding the volume and surface area of: cubes, cuboids, prisms, cylinders, spheres, pyramids, cones, and composite solids

9. Ratios

Ratio's are used to compare quantities or numbers. We use the symbol ':' to separate the numbers that are being compared. Let's say that I have 3 blue tiles, 8 red tiles and 5 yellow tiles. The ratio of blue tiles to red tiles is shown as 3:8. The ratio of blue tiles to red tiles to yellow tiles 3:8:5.
